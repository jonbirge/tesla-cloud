Tesla Cloud FAQ

Getting Started

Q: What is Tesla Cloud?
Tesla Cloud is a comprehensive web application designed specifically for Tesla's in-car browser. It provides news, weather, navigation, and other essential features optimized for vehicle use. This is an educational father-son project and is not affiliated with Tesla, Inc.
Q: How do I use Tesla Cloud in my Tesla?
Simply navigate to teslas.cloud in your Tesla's web browser. For the best experience:
  • Bookmark the page using the star in the browser menu
  • Add the browser to your dock for easy access while driving
  • Allow location services for GPS-dependent features
  • Consider creating a user ID to sync settings across devices
Q: Is Tesla Cloud free to use?
Yes, Tesla Cloud is completely free to use. There are no subscriptions, premium features, or hidden costs. It's maintained as an educational project to benefit the Tesla community.

Dashboard Section

Q: What information does the Dashboard provide?
The Dashboard is your navigation hub featuring:
  • Relative Wind Indicator: A circular radar display showing wind direction relative to your vehicle's heading
  • GPS Coordinates: Current latitude and longitude with precision indicators
  • Altitude Display: Elevation above sea level
  • Vehicle Heading: Compass direction in degrees
  • Embedded TeslaWaze: Full navigation interface with real-time traffic
Q: What is TeslaWaze and how do I use it?
TeslaWaze is an embedded navigation service that provides turn-by-turn directions with real-time traffic data. You can search for destinations, get route suggestions, and view traffic conditions directly within Tesla Cloud. It's designed to complement your Tesla's built-in navigation.
Q: Why does the GPS data show "--" or seem inaccurate?
GPS data depends on location services being enabled in your Tesla's browser and having a clear view of satellites. If you see "--", try:
  • Refreshing the page to re-request location permissions
  • Moving to an area with better satellite reception
  • Ensuring location services are enabled in browser settings

News Section

Q: How does the News section work?
The News section aggregates headlines from multiple Tesla and automotive sources including:
  • Tesla news from major automotive publications
  • Electric vehicle industry updates
  • General tech news relevant to Tesla owners
  • Market updates and stock information
Articles are refreshed regularly and can be shared via email if you're logged in.
Q: Can I customize which news sources appear?
Yes! After logging in, you can customize your news feeds in the Settings section. You can enable or disable specific news sources, set update frequencies, and even configure email forwarding for news articles.
Q: How does news sharing work?
When logged in, you can share interesting articles via email. Click the share button on any article, and it will be sent to your configured email address. This feature requires you to have email forwarding enabled in your user settings.

Weather Section

Q: What weather information is available?
The Weather section provides comprehensive meteorological data:
  • Current Conditions: Temperature, humidity, wind speed and direction
  • Extended Forecast: 7-day outlook with highs and lows
  • Precipitation Radar: Real-time rain and snow radar imagery
  • Weather Alerts: Severe weather warnings for your area
  • Historical Data: Recent weather trends and patterns
Q: How accurate is the weather radar?
The precipitation radar uses data from national weather services and is updated every 15-30 minutes. It's designed to help you plan charging stops and routes around weather conditions. The radar shows both current conditions and short-term precipitation forecasts.
Q: Why do I see weather alerts in the navigation bar?
Weather alerts appear as red dots in the top navigation when severe weather conditions are detected in your area. These alerts help you stay informed about conditions that might affect driving safety or charging availability.

Network Section

Q: What does the Network section monitor?
The Network section provides real-time connectivity diagnostics:
  • Connection Status: Overall network health indicator
  • Latency Monitoring: Response times to various servers
  • Speed Tests: Download and upload speed measurements
  • Regional Performance: Network quality in different geographic areas
  • Historical Trends: Connection quality over time
Q: How do I interpret the network status indicators?
Network status is shown with color-coded indicators:
  • Green: Excellent connection (low latency, high speed)
  • Yellow: Good connection with minor limitations
  • Orange: Poor connection affecting some features
  • Gray: No connection or unable to test
The bar indicator in the top-right corner gives you an at-a-glance view of your current connection quality.

Tesla Cloud automatically detects when the vehicle is in motion and disables entertainment features that could distract the driver. This includes video streaming, games, and other interactive content. Audio-only features like music and podcasts remain available.

Settings & Account Management

Q: Do I need to create an account to use Tesla Cloud?
No account is required for basic functionality. All settings are saved locally in your browser by default. However, creating a user ID provides several benefits:
  • Sync settings across multiple devices and browsers
  • Access to premium features like news sharing
  • Persistent configuration even after browser resets
  • Enhanced customization options
Q: How do I create a user ID and sync settings?
To create a user ID:
  1. Click the "Login" button in the bottom-left corner
  2. Enter a unique ID of your choosing (9+ characters)
  3. Your settings will automatically sync to the cloud
  4. Use the same ID on other devices to access your settings
Your user ID can be anything memorable like your phone number or email address - it's encrypted before storage.
Q: What settings can I customize?
You can customize numerous aspects of Tesla Cloud:
  • Display: Dark/light mode, text size, layout preferences
  • News: Source selection, update frequency, email forwarding
  • Weather: Units (metric/imperial), alert preferences
  • Navigation: Default map service, route preferences
  • Privacy: Data sharing preferences, analytics opt-out
Q: What information is stored on your servers?
We follow a privacy-first approach and store minimal data:
  • Encrypted user ID (only if you create one)
  • Settings preferences associated with your ID
  • Email address (only if news forwarding is enabled)
  • No personal information, browsing history, or location data
  • No tracking or analytics beyond basic usage statistics

Troubleshooting & Technical Issues

Q: The GPS/location features aren't working. What should I do?
GPS issues are usually related to browser permissions:
  1. Refresh the page and allow location access when prompted
  2. Check if location services are enabled in your Tesla's browser settings
  3. Try moving to an area with better satellite reception
  4. Restart the browser application completely
  5. If problems persist, try clearing browser cache and cookies
Q: Some external links don't work or load slowly.
Tesla's browser has some limitations with external content:
  • Some websites may not be optimized for the Tesla browser
  • Complex sites with heavy JavaScript may load slowly
  • Certain plugins or media formats aren't supported
  • Network connectivity can affect loading times
Try refreshing the page or accessing the site at a different time if you experience issues.
Q: The app seems slow or unresponsive.
Performance issues can usually be resolved by:
  1. Refreshing the page (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
  2. Clearing browser cache and cookies
  3. Restarting Tesla's browser application
  4. Checking your network connection status
  5. Closing other browser tabs to free up memory
Tesla Cloud is designed to be lightweight, but external content may sometimes affect performance.
Q: Why do stock prices and some data show "--"?
Data showing as "--" typically indicates:
  • External APIs are temporarily unavailable
  • Network connectivity issues
  • Services requiring authentication are not configured
  • Regional restrictions on certain data sources
This is normal behavior and doesn't affect core Tesla Cloud functionality.
Q: How do I report bugs or suggest improvements?
We welcome feedback! You can:
  • Email us at feedback@teslas.cloud
  • Include specific details about the issue (what you were doing, error messages, etc.)
  • Mention your Tesla model and software version if relevant
  • Suggest new features or improvements
Please be patient with responses as this is a volunteer-maintained project.

Privacy & Security

Q: How is my data protected?
Tesla Cloud prioritizes your privacy:
  • No tracking: We don't use analytics or tracking scripts
  • Minimal data collection: Only essential settings are stored
  • Encryption: User IDs and settings are encrypted before storage
  • No data selling: We never share or sell user information
  • Local storage: Most data is stored locally in your browser
Q: Can I delete my account and data?
Yes, you can delete your account at any time:
  1. Go to Settings and log in with your user ID
  2. Click the account management options
  3. Select "Delete Account" to remove all server-stored data
  4. Clear your browser cache to remove local data
Account deletion is immediate and irreversible.
Q: Is Tesla Cloud safe to use while driving?
Tesla Cloud includes several safety features:
  • Entertainment features are automatically disabled while driving
  • Navigation and essential information remain accessible
  • Large, touch-friendly interface elements
  • Minimal driver distraction design
Always prioritize safe driving and use voice commands when possible.
